3D Art

A collection of the notable 3D work I did the last few years.

Overview

Here you will see my best 3D work I created as a student during my time at Howest DAE and beyond. I primarily use Autodesk Maya for modeling, but I am experienced with Blender as well. For texturing, I use Substance Painter and Photoshop. You will also see Unity VFX and Shader Graph shaders.

Robot Buddy

This round, friendly robot was made in Maya, animated in Blender, baked and textured in Substance Painter and presented in Unity.

Devil Kart

A racing kart with a devilish look. Made in Maya, animated in Blender, baked and textured in Substance Painter and presented in Unity.

EXR

Some of the structures and props I made for the fitness rowing app EXR during my internship at the game studio Salty Lemon. EXR uses a low poly art style to avoid issues with user devices.

The models include a bridge with a steam locomotive, forest animals, a military truck and buildings and an overgrown tunnel entrance. You can check the game out via the Released Games page.

Rustic Windmill

A sole windmill atop a hill, surrounded by colorful trees, bushes and flowers. The windmill was modeled in Maya, and has a modular design so variations could be made freely. Textures were done in Substance Painter. The shaders for the trees, bushes, water, etc. along with the terrain and particles were made with Unity.

Lava Castle

An evil lava castle that could house the final boss of a game. Modeled in Maya, textured in Substance Painter. The shaders, VFX, terrain and particles were made with Unity.

Museo Poseído

Some of the props I made for the game Museo Poseído, as part of a team of students at Howest DAE. Modeled in Maya, textures were done in Substance Painter.
